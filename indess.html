Database Design for Bridal Registry and College System

Performance Task 3.2 - Bridal Registry System ERD

A department store has a bridal registry. This registry keeps information about the customer (usually the bride), the products that the store carries, and the products for which each customer registers. Customers typically register for a large number of products, and many customers register for the same products.

Entities and Attributes

CUSTOMER
  • CustomerID (PK)
  • FirstName
  • LastName
  • ContactInfo
  • Email
  • Phone
  • Address
PRODUCT
  • ProductID (PK)
  • ProductName
  • Description
  • Category
  • Price
  • StockQuantity
REGISTRY
  • RegistryID (PK)
  • CustomerID (FK)
  • ProductID (FK)
  • RegistrationDate
  • QuantityRequested
  • Priority

Relationships

Customer to Registry: One-to-Many (One customer can have multiple registry entries)
Product to Registry: One-to-Many (One product can appear in multiple registry entries)
Customer to Product: Many-to-Many (Through the Registry associative entity)

College Grade Reporting System ERD

Based on the provided grade report, identify the entities and attributes for a college system for tracking students, courses, instructors, and grades.

Sample Grade Report

SAD22 SYSTEMS ANALYSIS AND DESIGN CRUZ RM523 A
AIS32 ACCOUNTING INFORMATION SYSTEM SANCHEZ RM511 D

Entities and Attributes

STUDENT
  • StudentID (PK)
  • Name
  • Campus
  • Major
  • Address
COURSE
  • CourseID (PK)
  • Title
  • InstructorName (FK)
INSTRUCTOR
  • InstructorName (PK)
  • InstructorLocation
ENROLLMENT
  • EnrollmentID (PK)
  • StudentID (FK)
  • CourseID (FK)
  • Grade
  • Semester
  • Year

Relationships

Student to Enrollment: One-to-Many (One student can enroll in multiple courses)
Course to Enrollment: One-to-Many (One course can have multiple students enrolled)
Instructor to Course: One-to-Many (One instructor can teach multiple courses)
Student to Course: Many-to-Many (Through the Enrollment associative entity)
